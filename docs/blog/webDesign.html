Why Is Web Design So Important?


During my readings, I've learnt that the most effective websites require a great deal of preparation. A great site requires planning long before a single line of code is typed. The process contains 6 key steps:

The first one is centered around information gathering. It includes understanding who the app is being developed for and the requirements required to deliver the project for the sponsor and their users. Often this entails developing a business requirements report or a business analysis case.

Planning is the second part which encapsulates taking the information gathered and developing a plan to develop the most effective web site possible. It is important to consider the resources, tools, and stakeholders that will be critical to building the website. It is also important to consider the assets and content that will go into the site.

Designing the site comes with understanding the layout. Paper prototypes might be developed along with wireframes, mockups, and eventually clickable prototypes or Wizard of Oz prototypes to be tested by selected test participants.

Development entails taking the information gathered from the previous steps to code out the site and have it ready to UX testing and testing of the solution itself. This might include white, grey, or black box testing or a mixture of several testing techniques.

The testing and delivery part entails rigorous user experience testing along with performance tests of the website itself. The site has to have a solid user interface and be functionally reliable for the user. It must be resilient against threats and be able to function when faced with cyber threats and user volumes.

Lastly, even when a site is built it must continually be maintained to keep it appealing with current design trends and to ensure that patches and updates are made to ensure that the site continues to serve the sponsor and their clients well.
