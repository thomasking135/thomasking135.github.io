Problem Solving With JS

Here are my responses to the problem solving activities



Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you:

1. Got blocked on a simple problem

"One embarrassing incident was when I added two numbers when I really wanted to concatenate two strings. I wanted to say that “2020” is my favourite form of cricket but it came out as an addition saying I like 40 cricket."

"Here was the problem:"

"const num1 = 20;"

"const num2 = 20;"

"console.log(num1 + num2 + “ is my favourite form of cricket!”)"

Solved a problem in an elegant way

"I solved the above problem by going online and searching for an answer on Stack Overflow. I was quickly able to understand where I had made the error and made the correction immediately."

"A similar error was when I was trying to concatenate an array. In the error I had two arrays:"

"Ar1 = [1,2,3,4,5]"

"Ar2 = [6,7,8]"

"When I entered:"

"Console.log(Ar1 + Ar2); the console returned back"

"[1,2,3,4,56,7,8]"

"To correct the error, I found the solution was to use the concatenation function: console.log(ar1.concat(ar2));"

"This returned the concatenated array correctly."

"[1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8]"

"In both cases the key to solving the problem was to step back and think through what I had done wrong. When I was confused, I started taking a proactive approach to search for answers."

What was the problem?

"The problem was an error I made while trying to concatenate two strings. Instead of concatenating (joining the strings) the values were numerically added."

What problem solving techniques did you use?

"I am always driven to finding my own solutions before calling on others. In the cases above I took time to look at the error messages and tried to problem solve looking at the feedback returned from the console. After that, I looked over my course notes. If that failed, I was straight onto Google."

How did you feel throughout the process?

"I enjoyed it even if there were some challenging moments. It was great when I was able to find solutions to an issue I was having."

What did you learn?

"I learned that there are a lot of resources out there which are of great value. I also learned that there are many friendly people who are only too happy to lend their assistance during my times of difficulty. "

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and process:

Pseudocode

"I think pseudocode is brilliant for providing the steps to planning an effective solution to addressing any coding project. It is like having a set of instructions to put together and Ikea furniture kitset."

Trying something

"Although it is not always the most efficient or effective solution, I often enjoy experimenting with the code to see if I can figure out a solution to a problem by myself. It allows me to experiment with considering a variety of logical possibilities which may work to solve a problem."

Rubber ducky method

"While I found this idea awkward to embrace at first, I like the idea of trying to explain a programme line by line in simple natural language to a rubber duck. It allowed me to see whether I understood the code or not. I think it is something I’m still trying to come to grasps in applying as a developer."

Reading error messages

"I became quite sound at using the debugger on VSC to check my code. I really enjoyed using the extension packages to assist me in improving my programming practices too."

Console.logging

"The web dev inspection tools were incredibly useful for testing my code. It was great to have an accessible environment where I could test and inspect my HTML, CSS, and Javascript coding at once. "

Googling

"As a proactive independent learner, Googling was an integral part of my learning. As I’m sure is the case with many people in my cohort, Google provided a fast and responsive way to address my most pressing questions. "

Asking your peers for help

"I was reluctant to ask for help at first out of the desire to squarely depend on my own abilities to search for answers. However, the Slack channel provided me with a wealth of eager peers only too willingly to assist me with my queries."

Asking coaches for help

"Again, I was hesitant about asking my facilitators for help for fear perhaps of being viewed as a lazy or even dumb student. However, I must see them as an invaluable tool to my learning as a budding programmer."

Improving your process with reflection

"Reflecting proved an invaluable way to step away from the problem and to come back and address it afresh. It was a hard approach for me to embrace at first. However, it has become an integral part of the way I address problems these days."
